Description

The requirements were simple: build a contraption that simulates a satellite moving on its orbital path at different elevations. This was a summer break project and the time was therefore limited. Just two months to figure out a concept, do the design, manufacture the parts, assemble, test and tune the system to have it ready for the upcoming open day of the university.

The concept environed a U-shaped rail with a tilting mechanism to change the elevation of the orbit. A carriage would need to move on that rail to simulate the 'satellite' on its orbital track.

This ultimately led to the modular [laser-cut] construction made from several arc segments forming a semicircle. A belt was driven along the arc by a small stepper motor and [its two-stage gearbox] (inspired by cable lifts), that [dragged the carriage] along with it. Steel counterweights at each end kept the torque required to change the orbit's elevation to a minimum.

Underneath the carriage, an ArUco served as a target for the ground station to track. The position of the marker was extracted with OpenCV and a simple PID loop ensured smooth tracking.

Lastly, a Qt/QML [interface] was added to give guests the possibility to take manual control over the antenna and the orbit. It also served as a convenient interface to tweak the gains of the PID loop.

This project was exhibited during several open days until receiving significant damage during transport.
